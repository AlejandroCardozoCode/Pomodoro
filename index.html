What it is?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. The methodology is based on focused work intervals, known as "pomodoros," lasting 25 minutes, followed by short breaks. After four pomodoros, a longer break is taken. This technique promotes productivity by breaking work into manageable segments, enhancing concentration, and reducing mental fatigue. The name comes from the kitchen timer shaped like a tomato (pomodoro in Italian) that Cirillo used during his studies.
